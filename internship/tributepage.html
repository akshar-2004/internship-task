Tribute to Nikola Tesla

Born: July 10, 1856 | Died: January 7, 1943

Best Invention: Alternating Current (AC)

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electricity supply system. His pioneering work laid the foundation for numerous technological advancements in the field of electrical engineering.

Tesla's inventions and theoretical work formed the basis of wireless communication and radio, contributing to the development of a wide range of technologies that continue to impact our daily lives. Despite facing many challenges and financial difficulties, his visionary ideas and dedication to scientific exploration have earned him a lasting legacy as one of the greatest inventors in history.

Tesla's innovative mind and relentless pursuit of knowledge continue to inspire scientists, engineers, and inventors around the world. His work has not only shaped our understanding of electricity and magnetism but also opened new avenues for exploration in the realm of physics and engineering.

In memory of Nikola Tesla - A true visionary and pioneer in electrical engineering
